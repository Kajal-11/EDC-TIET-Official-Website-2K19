Our Collaborations

Entrepreneurship Development Cell is a group, that since it's inception, has tried and succeeded in bringing out the inner entrepreneurs of many a youngsters and gave them the push necessary to overcome the inertia that has kept them from starting up. But this year, we hope to something even more important - to make sure that children reach the stage where they are in a position to become the next business tycoon. To accomplish this, EDC in association with Rotary Club and UNICEF will be organising various events to help the less fortunate children in Patiala.
Rotary International is a voluntary nonprofit organization whose stated purpose is to bring together leaders of business and various influential people in order to provide humanitarian service and to advance goodwill and peace around the world. At this time, more than a million members of 35000+ Rotary International affiliated clubs proudly call themselves Rotarians. Their motto is “Service Above Self" and they are committed to creating inroads with people so that opportunities to serve will arise from those connections. The Rotarian philosophy is that mutual service is the best way to create thriving businesses and societies.
The United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF), originally known as the United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund was created to provide emergency food and healthcare to children and mothers in countries that had been devasted in World War II. But soon after, it's mandate was extended to address the long-term needs of children and women in developing countries everywhere. In 1953 it became a permanent part of the United Nations, and the words "international" and "emergency" were dropped from the organization's name, though it retained the original acronym, "UNICEF". UNICEF works in over 190 countries and territories to save children’s lives, to defend their rights, and to help them fulfil their potential, from early childhood through adolescence. It also promotes policies and expands access to services that protect all children.
